German V2

Historically relevant information about the V2

The V2 was a missile developed by Nazi Germany during WW2. Its success had many effects, one of which was the US & USSR developing their own missiles.

Development and Use

Developed by Nazi Germany in the late 1930s to early 1940s. The V2 was a missile intended to target nearby, but farther away than most weapons could get to, targets.

The majority of V2s were build and launched in and around the city of Pennemunde, on the west coast of Germany.

The lead designer and engineer of the project was Werner Von Braun, the same person behind the Saturn 5

End

After the end of WW2 the V2 was used by the US and USSR to make their own Missiles, and later on rockets as well.
