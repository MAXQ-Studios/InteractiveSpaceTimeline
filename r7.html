R-7 Information

Historically relevant information on the R-7 Missile / Rocket

The R-7 was an Intercontinental ballistic missile developed by the Soviet Union in the 1950s. It would later on launch Sputnik, but also be developed into the Soyuz series of launchers, one of the oldest lines of rockets today.

Development

The R-7 was developed by the main rocket/missile organization of the Soviet Union, OKB-1, and would become the first Intercontinental Ballistic Missile.

R-7, like its current Soyuz decedent, uses a series of boosters on the first stage to assist in ascent. The seperation of the boosters has been nicknamed the "Korolev Cross," named after the lead engineer, Sergei Korolev.

Use

Missile

The R-7 and its variants would be used as a major missile system of the USSR until it was phased out in 1968 cause it was not very good at being a missile

Rocket

A modified R-7 was used to launch Sputnik later the year of the first flight of the normal R-7. It would also evolve into Korolev's Soyuz rocket which varients of are still used today.
